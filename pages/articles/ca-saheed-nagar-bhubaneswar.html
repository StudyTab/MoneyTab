What Services Do Small Businesses Actually Need from a CA?

Before picking a CA, know what you actually need. For most small businesses and startups in Bhubaneswar, the core services are:

  • GST Registration — mandatory if annual turnover exceeds ₹20 lakh (₹10 lakh for special category states)
  • GST Return Filing — monthly (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B) or quarterly filing under QRMP scheme
  • Income Tax Return (ITR) Filing — annual filing for the business and proprietor
  • TDS Compliance — if you have employees or make payments above threshold limits
  • Bookkeeping & Accounting — maintaining proper books for bank loans and audits
  • Business Registration — Proprietorship, Partnership, LLP, or Pvt Ltd Company
  • MSME / Udyam Registration — to access government schemes and priority lending
  • PF & ESIC Filing — if you have 10 or more employees
  • Audit — mandatory for businesses above ₹1 crore turnover

A single good CA can handle all of this. A great one will proactively remind you about deadlines before penalties hit.

Documents to Bring for Your First CA Visit

Whether you’re registering a new business or sorting out existing compliance, bring these to your first meeting:

For the Business Owner / Proprietor

  • Aadhaar Card — self-attested copy (mandatory)
  • PAN Card — business owner’s personal PAN
  • Passport-size photographs — 2 copies
  • Mobile number linked to Aadhaar — needed for GST OTP verification

For Business Registration / GST Setup

  • Business PAN (if already registered as a company or LLP)
  • Proof of business address — electricity bill, property tax receipt, or rent agreement
  • Bank account details — cancelled cheque or passbook first page
  • Nature of business & approximate annual turnover

For Existing Businesses Needing Compliance Help

  • Last year’s ITR acknowledgement (if filed)
  • GST username and password or your GSTIN number
  • Last 6 months’ bank statements (business account)
  • Purchase and sales invoices for the relevant period
Note: You do not need to bring everything perfectly organised on the first visit. A good CA will tell you exactly what they need. Bringing whatever you have is enough to get started.

5 Questions to Ask Before Hiring a CA

  1. 1
    Are you a registered CA with ICAI membership? Every practicing CA in India must be a member of the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI). You can verify their membership number at icai.org. Don’t be shy about asking — any genuine CA will be happy to share their membership number.
  2. 2
    Have you handled businesses in my type of trade before? A CA who regularly works with retail traders understands Odisha commercial tax nuances differently from one who mainly handles IT companies or NGOs. Industry familiarity matters a lot for accurate advice.
  3. 3
    Who will actually do my work — you or a junior? In some firms, the senior CA signs off but the work is done by an article student (trainee). This is legal and common, but you should know upfront so you can call the right person when you have a question.
  4. 4
    What is your turnaround time for GST return filing? GST deadlines are strict — late filing attracts ₹50/day in late fees (₹20/day for NIL returns) and interest at 18% per annum on outstanding tax. Ask if they send you a reminder before the due date.
  5. 5
    Do you provide a written engagement letter? A professional CA firm will give you a letter outlining services, fees, and responsibilities. This protects both parties and is a sign of a properly run practice.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a CA and a Tax Consultant?
A Chartered Accountant (CA) is a licensed professional registered with ICAI who has passed the CA Final exam and completed 3 years of articleship. They are legally permitted to sign statutory audit reports. A Tax Consultant is typically a commerce graduate who handles GST and income tax filing but is not a licensed CA. For businesses below ₹1 crore turnover, a good tax consultant handles day-to-day compliance just as effectively — and often at a lower cost. Above ₹1 crore, a CA’s signature becomes mandatory for audits.
Can I change my CA if I’m not satisfied?
Yes, absolutely. There is no lock-in when it comes to CA services. You will need to get your login credentials, GST username and password, and copies of all filed returns from your existing CA. Under ICAI guidelines, a CA must cooperate in the handover to a successor CA. Most transitions are smooth if you communicate professionally.
What happens if my GST return is filed late?
Late GST return filing attracts a penalty of ₹50 per day (₹20/day for NIL returns), capped at ₹5,000 per return. Additionally, interest at 18% per annum is charged on any outstanding tax liability. If you miss multiple returns, your GST registration can be suspended or cancelled, which means you cannot legally issue GST invoices until it is restored.
Do I need a CA if my turnover is below ₹20 lakh?
If your annual turnover is below ₹20 lakh, GST registration is generally not mandatory (except for inter-state supply, e-commerce, or certain specific businesses). However, you still need to file an annual ITR. A CA or tax consultant can help you even at this stage — especially for business registration, MSME/Udyam registration, and ensuring your books are ready if your turnover crosses the threshold in future.
Is the first consultation at these CA firms free?
Most CA firms in Saheed Nagar offer a free first consultation where they assess your requirements and give you a fee quote. This is standard practice. Bring your documents, explain your business, and ask for a written quote. You are under no obligation until you sign an engagement letter.
Can these CA firms handle income tax for salaried employees too?
Yes. All five firms listed handle ITR filing for individuals, salaried employees, and business owners. If you or a family member needs ITR-1 or ITR-2 filing, these firms can do it — typically for ₹500–₹2,000 depending on complexity.
