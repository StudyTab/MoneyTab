Documents Required for ITR Filing

Carry these to any of the offices below — showing up without Form 16 will cost you a second trip.

For Salaried Individuals

  • Form 16 — from your employer, mandatory
  • PAN Card — original + photocopy
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Bank passbook / statement — for interest income and refund credit
  • Previous year’s ITR acknowledgement — if any
  • Investment proofs — LIC, PPF, ELSS, home loan certificate (for 80C/80D deductions)

For Self-Employed / Freelancers / Business Owners

All of the above, plus:

  • Bank statements — all accounts, full year
  • GST returns — if registered
  • TDS certificates — Form 26AS (download from income tax portal)
  • P&L statement or income summary
  • Rental agreements — if property income is involved

For Capital Gains (Stocks / Mutual Funds)

  • Broker’s capital gains statement — annual, available from Zerodha, Groww, Angel One, etc.
  • Purchase / sale price records

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I file ITR even if my income is below ₹2.5 lakh?
Yes — and in some cases you should. If TDS was deducted from your salary or interest income, filing is the only way to claim a refund. There is no penalty for filing voluntarily even below the exemption limit.
How long does it take to get the refund after filing?
Typically 15–45 days after e-verification. Refunds are credited directly to the bank account linked to your PAN. If you file early (before June), refunds tend to come faster as the department’s queue is shorter.
What if I missed filing for a previous year?
You can file a belated return up to 31st December of the same assessment year, or an updated return (ITR-U) up to 2 years later — with a penalty of ₹1,000–₹5,000 depending on income. All five offices in this list handle belated filings.
Is Form 16 mandatory?
It’s not legally mandatory, but without it your CA will have to reconstruct your salary breakdown from payslips and your Form 26AS. Possible — just slower and slightly more expensive. Always ask your employer for Form 16 before your CA visit.
Should I use online portals (ClearTax, etc.) or a local CA?
Online portals work well for simple salaried returns with no deductions or capital gains to track. The moment you have business income, RSUs, HRA disputes, or a tax notice — a local CA who can respond to an Income Tax Department query on your behalf is worth every rupee.
What is the penalty for filing late?
Under Section 234F: ₹1,000 if your gross total income is below ₹5 lakh; ₹5,000 if income exceeds ₹5 lakh. Additionally, you lose the right to carry forward certain losses (like capital losses) if you file after the due date.
