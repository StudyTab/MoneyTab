Documents You Need for GST Registration

Do not contact any agent without having these ready. Missing even one document adds 2–3 days to your timeline.

For Proprietorship (Sole Trader / Freelancer)

  • PAN Card of the owner — mandatory, no exceptions
  • Aadhaar Card of the owner (linked to mobile for OTP)
  • Passport-size photograph (white background, recent)
  • Business address proof — electricity bill / rent agreement / NOC from property owner
  • Bank account proof — cancelled cheque or bank statement with account number and IFSC
  • Nature of business / HSN or SAC code — your agent will help with this

For Private Limited Company

  • Certificate of Incorporation (from MCA)
  • MOA and AOA
  • Board resolution authorising the applicant to register for GST
  • PAN of the company (separate from director PAN)
  • Registered office address proof
  • All identity and address proofs for each director (PAN, Aadhaar, photo)

For Partnership Firm / LLP

  • PAN and Aadhaar of all partners
  • Partnership deed (registered or unregistered)
  • Address proof of place of business
  • Bank account proof in the firm’s name

Additional for E-Commerce Sellers

If you’re selling on Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, or any online marketplace — GST registration is mandatory regardless of turnover. You’ll additionally need the HSN code of products and marketplace enrolment confirmation (seller ID or dashboard screenshot).

The GST Registration Process — Step by Step

1

DSC or Aadhaar OTP

Companies and LLPs need a Class 3 DSC. Proprietors can use Aadhaar OTP — faster and no DSC cost. S2S Biz Solutions in IRC Village can issue a Class 3 DSC in 2 hours if you need one.

2

Application Part A on gst.gov.in

Email, mobile, and PAN verification. A Temporary Reference Number (TRN) is generated. Valid for 15 days — Part B must be completed within this window.

3

Application Part B

Business details, address proof, HSN/SAC codes, bank details, authorised signatory — all uploaded here. This is where incorrect entries cause officer queries. A good agent fills this right the first time.

4

ARN Generated

Application Reference Number issued immediately. The GST officer has 7 working days to approve or raise a query. Track status at gst.gov.in › Track Application Status.

5

Officer Verification & Query (If Raised)

If queries are raised — typically about address proof or business description — you have 7 more working days to reply. Missing this window resets the entire application.

6

GSTIN Issued

15-digit GSTIN issued. Certificate downloadable from the portal. Total timeline: 3–14 working days for straightforward applications. Officer queries add 1–2 weeks.

💡 Local Tip: Beyond 10 working days with no ARN update, your agent needs to raise a grievance on the GST portal or call the jurisdictional office. Don’t wait passively. A good agent tracks this automatically — it’s part of what you’re paying for.

Getting Your Business Address Right

The address on your GST certificate must be a place of business you physically control — owned, rented, or with a written NOC from the property owner.

In Jayadev Vihar, most early-stage startups register from a home address, shared workspace / coworking space, or rented shop or small office.

  • Home address: Electricity bill of the property + signed NOC from the property owner if you’re a tenant. Agents have standard NOC templates.
  • Coworking space: The coworking operator usually issues a Letter of Authorisation or NOC. Ask them before applying — some have the GST address arrangement already built into their membership.
GST officers in Bhubaneswar have been cross-checking addresses since 2023 and may trigger a physical verification visit for residential address registrations. Keep the address consistent across PAN, Aadhaar, and bank account to avoid mismatches.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does GST registration take in Bhubaneswar?
3–7 working days if documents are clean and no query is raised. Up to 14 working days if a GST officer issues a clarification notice. The agents in Jayadev Vihar with higher review counts (S2S, M.K. Jena) tend to file complete applications that don’t trigger queries.
Can I do GST registration myself without an agent?
Yes — gst.gov.in is public. If you’re comfortable with HSN code selection and document uploads, you can do it yourself at zero cost. An agent’s value is in getting it right the first time without triggering a query that adds 1–2 weeks to your timeline.
Can I register for GST before my company is incorporated?
For proprietorship — yes, registration is in your personal name and no company structure is required. For Pvt Ltd — company incorporation must happen first; you’ll need the Certificate of Incorporation before applying for GST.
What is an ARN and why is my application taking so long?
ARN is the Application Reference Number — issued immediately after Part B submission. The officer has 7 working days to act. Beyond 10 working days with no update, your agent needs to raise a grievance on the GST portal or call the jurisdictional office. A passive agent who doesn’t follow up is a red flag.
Do I need separate GSTIN for each state I operate in?
Yes. GST is state-specific. An Odisha GSTIN doesn’t cover operations, warehousing, or supply originating in another state. If you expand outside Odisha, you’ll need a separate GSTIN for that state.
What if I don’t register and my turnover crosses the threshold?
Penalty of 10% of tax due, minimum ₹10,000 — plus all GST you should have collected. Retrospective registration is possible but creates a painful cleanup. Register proactively as you approach the threshold, not after crossing it.
Can a registration agent handle GST notice responses?
For basic clarification queries during the registration window — yes. For scrutiny notices, demand notices, or ITC mismatch issues post-registration — a CA or tax advocate is the right person. S K Pani & Co and M.K. Jena & Co can handle these; a registration-only agent like Swastika cannot.
