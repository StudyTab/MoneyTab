Most families in Bhubaneswar treat an education loan for abroad studies as a bank problem. It is not. It is a paperwork and sequencing problem. The bank’s role is the last 20%. The first 80% is choosing the right destination country, getting into a university the lender recognises, understanding what collateral versus non-collateral loans look like, and building the file before stepping into any branch.

Education loans for abroad studies in India run from ₹7.5 lakh under the Central Scheme (Government of India, zero processing fee, interest subsidy for EWS applicants) up to ₹1.5 crore for unsecured loans from private NBFCs like Credila and Avanse. The rate difference between the cheapest and most expensive lender for the same student profile can be 4–5 percentage points per year. On ₹40 lakh over 10 years, that is ₹8–12 lakh in extra interest.

The lender you approach first — and whether you approach them directly or through a consultant — sets the baseline for everything that follows.

Study Abroad Consultants in Bhubaneswar

A good study abroad consultant does more than find you a university. They sequence your IELTS / GRE / GMAT prep, shortlist universities that match both your academic profile and your loan eligibility, prepare your SOP and LOR, and — crucially — guide you through the loan documentation stack. Bad consultants either don’t understand loans at all or push you toward lenders who pay them referral fees.

Documents Required — Education Loan for Abroad Studies

Carrying an incomplete document set is the single most common reason for delay. Prepare this list before approaching any lender or consultant.

Student Documents

  • PAN Card (mandatory)
  • Aadhaar Card
  • 10th and 12th mark sheets and passing certificates
  • Graduation certificates and transcripts (all semesters)
  • Admission letter from foreign university (confirmed offer, not conditional)
  • Course fee structure from university (official letter or fee schedule)
  • IELTS / TOEFL / GRE / GMAT scorecard (wherever applicable)
  • Visa copy (if already received — some lenders require it for disbursement)

Co-Applicant (Parent / Guardian) Documents

  • PAN Card
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Income proof: Last 3 months’ salary slips + Form 16 (salaried) OR ITR with CA certification for last 3 years (self-employed)
  • Bank statements — last 12 months (salary account)
  • Employment certificate / appointment letter
  • Property documents (if collateral is being offered)

Collateral Documents (if applicable)

  • Sale deed / title deed of the property
  • Encumbrance certificate (last 13 years)
  • Valuation report from a bank-approved valuer
  • Property tax receipt (latest)
  • NOC from co-owners (if property has multiple owners)

Additional Documents

  • 2 passport-size photographs (student + co-applicant)
  • Passport copies (student and co-applicant)
  • University QS ranking printout (for SBI Global Ed-Vantage — keep a copy)

Central Government Schemes — Interest Subsidy

🏫 Dr. Ambedkar Central Sector Scheme
For students from OBC and EBC categories. Interest subsidy during moratorium period on loans up to ₹20 lakh from scheduled banks. Apply at jansamarth.in.
🏫 Padho Pardesh Scheme
For students from minority communities. Interest subsidy on education loans for abroad studies up to ₹20 lakh. Apply through the Minority Affairs Ministry portal linked at jansamarth.in.
🏫 Central Scheme for Interest Subsidy (CSIS)
For economically weaker sections (family income below ₹4.5 lakh). Full interest subsidy during moratorium for loans taken under IBA model education loan scheme. Verify eligibility and apply at jansamarth.in — the Government of India’s unified portal for all education loan schemes.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I apply for the education loan?
Start the loan file 3–4 months before your visa appointment. The i20, CAS, or offer letter is usually needed before the bank processes the file, so begin the moment you receive a conditional offer. The document preparation alone — getting ITR, property valuation, encumbrance certificates — takes 4–6 weeks if you start from zero.
Is a co-applicant mandatory?
For SBI and most public sector banks — yes. A parent or guardian must co-sign. Their income determines the repayment capacity calculation. For NBFCs like Credila and Avanse — technically not mandatory, but in practice they ask for one if you’re a recent graduate without income.
Can I get a loan without collateral for a US master’s programme?
Yes — Credila and Avanse offer collateral-free loans up to ₹75 lakh for select universities. The interest rate will be 11–14%. SBI’s unsecured limit is ₹7.5 lakh, which is rarely enough for a US programme. If your US university is on Credila’s pre-approved list, that is your fastest collateral-free path in Bhubaneswar.
My university is not on SBI’s approved list. What do I do?
Apply to Credila or Avanse — their university lists are much broader. Alternatively, check Bank of Baroda’s Baroda Scholar scheme, which has its own list. A good consultant will know which lender covers your specific institution. This is exactly the kind of question where paying a consultant’s fee saves you weeks of misdirected effort.
Does the education loan cover living expenses?
Yes — the IBA model for education loans covers: tuition, hostel, travel, books and equipment, insurance, and a caution deposit or study tour. Confirm the specific components your bank will disburse. Some banks disburse directly to the university and keep remaining funds in a linked account; others give you access after disbursement milestones.
What is the moratorium period?
Duration of the course plus 6 months (SBI) or 12 months (most NBFCs) after the course ends or the student finds employment — whichever comes first. Interest accrues during this period, so your actual repayment amount is higher than what was disbursed. Ask the lender for a projected repayment schedule at sanction stage to understand the full cost.
Does the interest paid on an education loan give any tax benefit?
Yes — Section 80E of the Income Tax Act allows full deduction of interest paid for up to 8 years, with no upper cap on the amount. The benefit applies to whoever is actually paying the EMI — often the parent who is the co-applicant. For a salaried parent in the 30% tax bracket paying ₹3 lakh per year in interest, this is ₹90,000 back in their hands.
