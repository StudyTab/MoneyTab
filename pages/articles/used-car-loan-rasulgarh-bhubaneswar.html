What Is a Down Payment on a Used Car Loan?

When you buy a used car with a loan, the lender doesn’t finance 100% of the car’s value. The portion you pay from your own pocket on the day of purchase is the down payment, also called margin money or own contribution.

Car’s assessed value₹5,00,000
Lender finances 85% (LTV)₹4,25,000
You pay 15% upfront₹75,000
Lower LTV (70%)₹1,50,000 upfront

The lower the down payment percentage a lender requires, the less cash you need on Day 1. But lower down payment means higher EMIs, because you’re borrowing more.

Used cars vs new cars: For new cars, banks often finance 90–100% of on-road price. For used cars, it’s almost always lower — typically 70–90% depending on car age, condition, and lender policy. The down payment requirement on used cars varies significantly between lenders, which is why comparing lenders matters.

Why Down Payment on Used Cars Is Higher Than People Expect

Banks are cautious with used car loans for a simple reason: a used car depreciates faster than a new one, and if you default, the bank’s collateral, your car, loses value quickly. Key factors affecting your required down payment:

  • Car age: A 2020 car gets better financing than a 2016 car. Older cars get lower LTV.
  • Car model: Maruti Swift, Hyundai i20, Honda City, popular, resaleable models get better LTV. Unusual models get lower financing.
  • CIBIL score: Above 750 gets you 85-90% LTV. Below 650, you may be limited to 70–75%.
  • Income stability: Salaried employees with payslips get better terms than informal workers.
  • Loan tenure: Most lenders cap used car tenure at 5 years, and the combined age (car + loan term) usually can’t exceed 10–15 years.

Documents You Need for a Used Car Loan

Bring originals plus two self-attested photocopies of each:

Identity & Address

  • Aadhaar Card, buyer (mandatory for all loan applications)
  • PAN Card, mandatory for all loan applications
  • Passport-size photographs, 2 recent colour copies

Income Proof

  • Salaried: Last 3 months’ salary slips + Form 16 (or ITR for last 2 years)
  • Self-employed: Last 2 years’ ITR with computation + CA-certified financials
  • Last 6 months’ bank statements, for all applicants

Car-Specific Documents

  • RC (Registration Certificate), in seller’s name; must be clear with no active hypothecation
  • Insurance Certificate, valid and transferable to new owner
  • Form 29 & Form 30, signed by seller (RTO transfer forms)
  • NOC from previous bank, if the car had a previous loan still on the RC
  • Original purchase invoice or dealer quotation, showing car’s agreed price

Bank-Specific Additions

  • NACH/ECS mandate form, for EMI auto-debit setup (bank will provide)
  • Post-dated cheques (PDCs), some NBFCs (Mahindra Finance) require 3–5 PDCs
The single most important document: The car’s clear RC with no hypothecation — or a valid NOC from the previous lender if there was a previous loan. Without this, no bank will proceed. Always check the RC before agreeing to purchase price.

5 Strategies to Minimise Your Down Payment

  1. 1
    Apply as an existing customer Banks offer lower down payment requirements to customers who already have a savings or salary account. SBI and Axis Bank are particularly known for this advantage in Rasulgarh, existing account holders can sometimes unlock 85–90% LTV.
  2. 2
    Improve your CIBIL score before applying A score above 750 can unlock 85–90% LTV with banks. If your score is currently 680–710, spend 3–6 months paying down existing EMIs and credit card balances. The down payment difference on a ₹5 lakh car can be ₹50,000–₹75,000 lower.
  3. 3
    Choose a popular car model Banks finance popular cars at higher LTV because they’re easier to repossess and resell. In Bhubaneswar’s used market, Maruti Swift, Hyundai i20, Honda City, Maruti Baleno consistently get the best financing terms.
  4. 4
    Choose a newer car (within the last 4–5 years) A 2021 car will get 85% LTV financing. A 2016 car of the same model may only get 70–75%. Every additional year of age typically reduces the LTV by 2–5%.
  5. 5
    Use the dealer’s in-house financing tie-up, but compare independently Dealers like CarBaazar and CarpleX have direct tie-ups with HDFC, Axis, and Mahindra Finance. These sometimes include promotional low-down-payment offers on specific inventory. Ask the dealer: “Ke lender tie-up achi? Down payment kete lagibe?”, but also apply independently at SBI to compare rates.

Red Flags: What to Watch Out For in Rasulgarh’s Used Car Market

  • Inflated car valuation by dealer, Some dealers inflate the invoice price to help you borrow more. Banks have their own valuators. If the bank’s valuation comes lower than the invoice, you’ll need to cover the difference from your own pocket on top of the down payment.
  • NOC issues on the RC, If the previous owner had a car loan and didn’t get the bank’s NOC removed, you cannot get a new loan on that car. Always ask: “RC me hypothecation clear achi ki?”
  • Odometer tampering, A car showing 40,000 km that actually has 1,40,000 km will get a bank valuation far below the dealer’s asking price. Use OBD2 scanners at service centres near Mancheswar to verify actual mileage before finalising.
  • Insurance lapse, Banks require valid insurance as a condition of the loan. Some used cars in Rasulgarh lots have expired insurance. You’ll need to buy fresh insurance before the bank will disburse.

Frequently Asked Questions

What CIBIL score do I need for a used car loan here?
For banks (SBI, HDFC, Axis), the recommended minimum is 700–720 for the best LTV terms. Scores between 650–700 may still get approved but at lower LTV (higher down payment) and higher rates. Below 650, banks typically reject the application — Mahindra Finance or Shriram Finance are better options in that case, as they evaluate applications individually without a rigid CIBIL minimum.
Can I get a loan for a car older than 10 years?
Most banks (SBI, HDFC, Axis) cap used car financing at cars that are 10 years or newer at the time of loan origination. For older cars (10–15 years), Shriram Finance is the main option in Bhubaneswar — they finance cars up to 15 years old in some cases. The down payment will be higher and the rate will be 18–24% p.a. for older vehicles.
How long does approval take at these branches?
SBI typically takes 3–7 working days after document submission. HDFC Bank can approve in 1–3 working days for existing customers or those with pre-approved offers. Axis Bank GGP Colony branch is known for 24–48 hour processing for existing account holders. Mahindra Finance often processes same-day or next-day for standard cases. Their flexibility in eligibility comes with faster turnaround at the cost of higher rates.
Does the bank inspect the car before disbursing the loan?
Yes. Every lender sends a vehicle inspector / valuator to physically assess the car before disbursal. The valuator checks the car’s condition, verifies the odometer reading (though tampering is possible), confirms the chassis and engine numbers match the RC, and gives an assessed market value. The loan is then calculated as a percentage of this assessed value — not the dealer’s asking price. This valuation can sometimes come lower than expected, increasing your effective down payment.
Can I foreclose a used car loan early to save on interest?
Yes, but check the foreclosure charges first. SBI has no prepayment penalty after 12 months — the best option if you expect to foreclose early. HDFC charges 6% of outstanding principal in year 1, dropping to 3% after 24 months. Axis Bank has similar sliding charges. Mahindra Finance and Shriram Finance typically charge 2–5% regardless of when you close — less favourable for early closures. Always calculate whether the interest saving exceeds the foreclosure charge before prepaying.
What's the smartest sequence for a Rasulgarh buyer?
Visit CarBaazar or Nityananda Motors on the Bypass first — choose your car and get the dealer’s price and all car documents. Then walk into SBI Palasuni or Axis GGP Colony and apply for the loan independently. You get better rates than going through the dealer’s in-house financer, while still getting the car you want. Only fall back to the dealer’s NBFC tie-up if banks reject your application.
